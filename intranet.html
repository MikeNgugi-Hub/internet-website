Definition

An intranet consists of web pages and resources that are only available inside an organization. It is a private network that uses the same technologies as the Internet but is restricted to internal use only. Employees within the organization can access the intranet, but members of the public cannot.

Purpose of an Intranet

Organizations use intranets to share information, resources and tools internally in a secure and controlled environment. Common uses include:

  • Sharing internal company news and announcements
  • Storing and accessing company documents and policies
  • Internal communication between employees and departments
  • Accessing internal systems such as HR portals and finance tools
  • Collaborating on projects within the organization

Intranet vs The Internet

While the Internet is a public global network accessible by anyone in the world, an intranet is a private network accessible only to authorized users within a specific organization. The intranet uses the same underlying web technologies such as HTTP, HTML and browsers, but operates within a closed and secure environment.

Real World Example

A real world example of an intranet is Strathmore University's internal student portal at https://sagana.strathmore.edu/ — this is only accessible to students and staff within the university and is not available to the general public on the Internet.
