What is Extranet

An extranet falls between the Internet and an organization's intranet. It is a controlled private network that allows selected outsiders such as customers, suppliers or other trading partners to access certain parts of an organization's internal network. Unlike an intranet which is strictly internal, an extranet extends limited access to trusted external parties.

Who Can Access an Extranet?

Access to an extranet is not open to the general public. Only selected and authorized external users are granted access. These may include:

  • Customers of the organization
  • Suppliers and vendors
  • Business partners and trading partners
  • Contractors or consultants working with the organization

Extranet vs Intranet vs Internet

It is important to understand how the three are different from each other:

  • Internet — a public global network accessible by anyone in the world with no restrictions.
  • Intranet — a private network accessible only by members inside the organization.
  • Extranet — sits between the two. It is private like an intranet but allows selected external parties to access specific resources.

Why Do Organizations Use Extranets?

Organizations use extranets to collaborate more efficiently with their external partners without exposing their entire internal network to the public Internet. Common uses include sharing order information with suppliers, giving customers access to their account details, and allowing partners to collaborate on shared projects securely.
